# Waste-to-Energy: Biogas vs. Compost

When going off-grid, your waste is a resource. But should you turn it into cooking gas (Biogas) or fertilizer (Compost)?

## 1. Biogas: The Anaerobic Alpha
Systems like HomeBiogas use **anaerobic digestion** to break down organic matter (food scraps and human waste) in the absence of oxygen. The result is methane gas for cooking and a liquid bio-fertilizer.
- **Pros:** Instant energy utility.
- **Cons:** Requires consistent ambient temperatures (>20°C/68°F) to keep the bacteria active.

## 2. Composting: The Aerobic Soil-Builder
Composting toilets rely on **aerobic decomposition** (with oxygen) to break down waste into safe, dry humus.
- **Pros:** Works in colder climates; much simpler mechanical design.
- **Cons:** No energy output; requires a "cure" time of 6-12 months before use.
