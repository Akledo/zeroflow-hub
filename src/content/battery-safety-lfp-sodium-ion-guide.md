## Introduction
As we transition to a local-energy-first lifestyle, the "engine room" of the modern home is moving indoors. Home battery backups are no longer just for utility closets; they are integrated into garages and home labs. However, the high-profile news of EV fires has left many asking: **Are lithium batteries safe for indoor use?**

The answer lies in the specific chemistry of the battery. In this guide, we’ll compare the physics of thermal runaway in NMC, LFP, and the emerging champion of safety: Sodium-Ion.

## The Physics of Thermal Runaway
To understand battery safety, you must understand **thermal runaway**. This is a feedback loop where an increase in temperature changes the conditions in a way that causes a further increase in temperature.

1. **NMC (Nickel Manganese Cobalt):** Common in electric cars and high-end power tools. While energy-dense, NMC releases oxygen when it overheats. This oxygen fuels the fire from within, making NMC fires almost impossible to extinguish with standard water or foam.
2. **LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate):** This is the industry standard for home storage (e.g., Tesla Powerwall 3, EcoFlow Delta Pro). LFP has a much higher thermal runaway threshold (approx. 270°C vs 210°C for NMC) and, crucially, **does not release oxygen** during a failure. It might smoke or vent gas, but it won't "self-oxidize" into a blowtorch.

## Sodium-Ion: The New Standard for Home Safety
If LFP is "safe," Sodium-Ion is "inert." Sodium-ion batteries use salt-based electrolytes that are significantly less flammable than lithium-based counterparts.
- **No Thermal Runaway:** In many lab nail-penetration tests, sodium-ion cells show zero temperature spike.
- **Deep Discharge Safety:** Unlike lithium, which can become unstable if discharged to 0V, sodium-ion can be drained completely for safe transport and storage.

## Safety Best Practices for Indoor Installation
Regardless of chemistry, any energy storage system with 10kWh+ of capacity requires respect:
- **Proper Ventilation:** Even "safe" batteries can vent gasses if the BMS (Battery Management System) fails.
- **Smart Monitoring:** Use a HEMS (Home Energy Management System) to monitor cell temperatures in real-time.
- **Physical Isolation:** If possible, mount your battery on a non-combustible surface (like a concrete garage floor or fire-rated drywall).

## Conclusion
Is it safe to sleep in a house with a 20kWh battery? If it’s LFP or Sodium-Ion, the answer is a resounding **yes**. The risks are statistically lower than the risks posed by your natural gas line or gasoline-powered car in the garage.
