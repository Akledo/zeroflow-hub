# The 2026 Automated Lab Maintenance Protocol

Efficiency in an automated lab is not just about the hardware; it's about the maintenance of the sensors and actuators that keep the environment stable. This protocol is designed for prosumers using AC Infinity, Mars Hydro, or custom PLC-based systems.

## Section A: Automated Mycology Labs

For those running high-output fruiting chambers, following this recursive checklist ensures zero-fail logic and optimal biological yield.

### Weekly Maintenance (Est: 10 mins)
*   **CO2 Sensor Calibration:** Verify the CO2 sensor baseline. Calibrate to 400ppm in fresh air to ensure your "Fresh Air Exchange" (FAE) triggers are accurate.
*   **Humidifier Audit:** Check ultrasonic humidifier disks for bio-film buildup. Always use distilled water to prevent mineral dust from clogging your HEPA filters.
*   **Filter Inspection:** Inspect HEPA intake pre-filters for dust saturation. Reduced airflow leads to increased temperature and stagnant air pockets.

### Monthly Deep-Dive (Est: 30 mins)
*   **Chamber Sterilization:** Deep clean the fruiting chamber with 70% Isopropyl Alcohol (IPA) or a 3% Hydrogen Peroxide (H2O2) solution.
*   **Fail-Safe Logic Test:** Manually trigger a "High Temp" or "Low Humidity" alarm to ensure your fail-safe logic (e.g., "If Temp > 80°F, Turn Exhaust Fan to Max") still executes correctly.
*   **Mechanical Cleaning:** Clean fan blades. Even a small amount of spore buildup on blades can significantly reduce the static pressure efficiency of your exhaust system.

### Quarterly Overhaul (Est: 1 hour)
*   **Carbon Filter Replacement:** Replace active carbon filters. Spore escape can lead to contamination in the rest of your home or facility.
*   **Firmware Updates:** Check for controller firmware updates. 2026 updates have improved Hysteresis logic, preventing rapid "on-off" cycling of equipment.

## Section B: Vertical Indoor Gardens (Hydroponics)

Whether you are using a Gardyn, Rise Garden, or LettuceGrow Farmstand, these steps prevent system crashes and plant toxicity.

### Weekly
*   **Nutrient Balancing:** Check Electrical Conductivity (EC). For leafy greens, aim for 1.2–1.8 mS/cm.
*   **Root Management:** Prune roots near pump intakes. "Root-choke" is the #1 cause of pump failure in vertical systems.
*   **pH Balancing:** Top off the reservoir and balance pH to 5.8–6.2.

### Monthly
*   **AI Vision Calibration:** Clean camera lenses (especially on Gardyn systems) to ensure the growth-tracking AI can accurately identify nutrient deficiencies.
*   **Reservoir Refresh:** Dump and replace the entire water reservoir to prevent "nutrient lockout" caused by mineral salt accumulation.

### Bi-Annually
*   **System Descaling:** Run the entire system for 2 hours with a food-grade citric acid solution to remove calcium buildup in the irrigation lines.
