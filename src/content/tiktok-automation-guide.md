- **ElevenLabs** (for high-quality narration)
- **Descript** (for bulk editing and subtitles)
- **ChatGPT or Claude** (for script generation)

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: Generate 30 Short-Form Scripts
Use an AI like ChatGPT to generate a batch of 30 short scripts (under 60 seconds) based on your niche. 
**Pro Tip:** Ask the AI to follow a "Hook-Value-CTA" structure to maximize retention.

### Step 2: Bulk Generate Voiceovers with ElevenLabs
Once you have your scripts, head over to **ElevenLabs**. Use their "Professional Voice Cloning" or high-quality pre-set voices to generate the audio for all 30 scripts in one session.
- Download each audio file with a clear name (e.g., `Video_01.mp3`).

### Step 3: Assemble and Subtitle in Descript
Import your audio files into **Descript**. Use the "Underlord" AI feature to automatically generate subtitles and remove filler words.
- Apply a "Template" to all videos to ensure consistent branding (fonts, colors, and layout).
- Use the "Studio Sound" feature to make your AI voiceover sound like it was recorded in a professional booth.

[CTA: Try Descript’s Automation Features]

### Step 4: Add Visuals and Export
You can use stock footage or AI-generated b-roll to accompany your narration. Once satisfied, export your videos in the 9:16 aspect ratio optimized for TikTok.

## Common Pitfalls & Troubleshooting
- **Issue 1: Quality vs. Quantity:** Don't sacrifice quality for sheer volume. Ensure your hook is strong enough to stop the scroll.
- **Issue 2: TikTok Guidelines:** Avoid using copyrighted music or controversial AI-generated content that might trigger the "Unoriginal Content" flag.
- **Issue 3: Repetitive Visuals:** If you use the same stock clips too often, your audience will lose interest. Mix in different visual styles.
