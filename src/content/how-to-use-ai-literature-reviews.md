# How to Use AI for Literature Reviews in Science

## Introduction
The traditional literature review process—searching through databases, downloading PDFs, and manually extracting data—is famously time-consuming. Learning **how to use AI for literature reviews in science** is no longer just a "hack"; it's a critical skill for any modern researcher.

## Prerequisites
- A **Consensus** account (Free or Premium)
- An **Elicit** account (Free or Plus)
- A clear research question or topic

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: Formulate and Search with Consensus
Start by turning your research topic into a specific question. Consensus is best for finding the high-level "scientific consensus" on a topic.

1. Navigate to **Consensus**.
2. Type your question (e.g., "What is the impact of microplastics on human gut health?").
3. Review the **Consensus Meter** to see the general direction of the literature.
4. Use the **Synthesize** feature to get a one-sentence summary of the top findings.

### Step 2: Analyze and Extract with Elicit
Once you have identified key papers, switch to Elicit for deep data extraction.

1. Open **Elicit** and use the "Search for papers" feature.
2. Import the most relevant papers found during Step 1.
3. Click **"Add Column"** to automatically extract specific information such as sample size, methodology, and outcomes.
4. Compare these data points in a structured table.

### Step 3: Synthesize and Draft
Now that you have a structured table of data, synthesize the findings into your draft.

1. Look for patterns in the Elicit table.
2. Use summaries from both tools to draft your narrative.
3. Verify AI-generated summaries against the original text.

## Common Pitfalls
- **AI Hallucinations:** Always check the source snippet.
- **Keyword Over-Reliance:** Use variations of your search terms.
- **Paywall Issues:** Use institutional login or tools like Unpaywall.

## Conclusion
Mastering AI for literature reviews allows you to move from "searching" to "thinking" much faster. By combining **Consensus** with **Elicit**, you can produce higher-quality reviews in a fraction of the time.
