# Home Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy (HBOT): Longevity Hardware Specs & ROI

Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy (HBOT) is no longer confined to hospitals treating decompression sickness. In 2026, it has become a cornerstone of the **Longevity Home Lab**, used by prosumers to accelerate recovery, improve cognitive function, and enhance mitochondrial health.

But buying a home HBOT system is a major technical investment. Choosing the wrong chamber or concentrator can render the therapy ineffective or, worse, unsafe.

## 1. The Physics of Hyperbarics: PSI and ATA
The effectiveness of HBOT is determined by **Atmospheric Absolute (ATA)**—the total pressure inside the chamber.
- **1.0 ATA:** Standard sea-level pressure.
- **1.3 ATA (4 PSI):** The standard for "Soft-Shell" portable chambers. Effective for anti-inflammation and recovery.
- **1.5+ ATA (7+ PSI):** Typically requires a "Hard-Shell" chamber. Used for deeper neurological and clinical-grade protocols.

For most home users, a **1.3 ATA soft-shell system** provides the best balance of cost, safety, and effectiveness.

## 2. Hardware Requirements: The Oxygen Concentrator
A chamber is only half the system. You need an **Oxygen Concentrator** to feed it.
- **Purity:** You must use a concentrator capable of 90-95% oxygen purity at the required flow rate.
- **Flow Rate (LPM):** Most home chambers require a **10 Liter Per Minute (10 LPM)** concentrator. Using a standard 5 LPM medical unit will result in insufficient oxygen saturation inside the pressurized environment.

## 4. ROI: Buying vs. Clinical Visits
Clinical HBOT sessions typically cost **$150 - $300 per hour**. 
- **The "Protocol" ROI:** A standard longevity protocol might involve 40 sessions.
- **Clinical Cost:** $8,000+.
- **Home System Cost:** $4,500 (Chamber + Concentrator).

By the end of your first protocol, the home system has already paid for itself. For families or athletes requiring 100+ sessions a year, the ROI is astronomical.
