# Geothermal Retrofits: Physics of the Backyard

The most common myth about geothermal (Ground Source Heat Pumps) is that you need 5 acres of land. In reality, a residential retrofit is a matter of drilling depth, not surface area.

## 1. Vertical vs. Horizontal Loops
For existing homes, **vertical loops** are the only viable path. A drilling rig (no larger than a delivery truck) bores two or three 300ft holes in a standard driveway or backyard. This utilizes the 55°F constant temperature of the earth's crust as a heat sink/source.

## 2. The Ductwork Barrier
The real friction in a geothermal retrofit isn't the drilling—it's the **sensible heat transfer**. Geothermal systems move higher volumes of air at lower temperatures than gas furnaces. You must verify that your existing ducts are sized correctly for the lower Delta-T.

## 3. ROI and the 30% Alpha
With the federal 30% tax credit, the typical $25,000 retrofit cost drops to $17,500. When compared to the rising cost of [Air Source Heat Pumps in extreme cold](https://github.com/Akledo/zeroflow-hub/tree/main/content/heat-pump-extreme-cold-buying-guide-2027.md), the 25-year reliability of geothermal makes it the superior long-term asset.
