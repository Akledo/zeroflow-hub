## Introduction
For coastal residents and offshore sailors, "Water, water everywhere, nor any drop to drink" is more than just a poem—it’s a survival reality. While standard carbon and ceramic filters can remove bacteria from a lake, they are useless against the high salinity of the ocean.

To turn seawater into life-saving drinking water, you need **Reverse Osmosis (RO)**. But can you really do this with a portable kit? In this guide, we’ll look at the physics of portable desalination and whether these "survival watermakers" are worth the investment.

## The Physics of Osmotic Pressure
Desalination is an energy-intensive process because you are fighting against **Osmotic Pressure**. To force seawater through a semi-permeable membrane and leave the salt behind, you must apply pressure greater than the ocean’s natural osmotic pressure (about 800-900 psi).

In a large-scale plant, this is easy. In a portable survival kit, this requires either extreme manual labor or a high-efficiency 12V motor combined with an **Energy Recovery Device (ERD)**.

## Survival Reality: Hand-Pump vs. Powered
### Hand-Pump Desalinators (e.g., Katadyn Survivor 06/35)
- **The Reality:** These are designed for life rafts. Pumping enough water for one person for one day requires hours of strenuous physical labor.
- **Output:** Approximately 1 to 4 liters per hour.
- **Best Use:** Emergency-only "ditch bags" for blue-water sailors.

### Portable Powered Watermakers (e.g., Rainman, Spectra Ventura)
- **The Reality:** These use 12V or 240V motors and are significantly more practical for home backup or van life.
- **Efficiency:** Units like the Rainman can produce 15–30 gallons (60–120 liters) per hour.
- **Energy Cost:** Typically requires 10-20 amps at 12V.

## Critical Maintenance: The "Pickling" Problem
You cannot simply use a desalinator once and put it back in the closet. The RO membranes are organic-compatible and will grow bacteria/mold within days if left "wet" without preservative.
- **Flushing:** After each use, you must flush the system with fresh water.
- **Pickling:** If storing for more than a week, you must circulate a chemical preservative (sodium metabisulfite) through the system.

## Conclusion
Portable desalination is a miracle of modern physics, but it is not "plug and play." If you live on the coast and want a resilient water backup, a powered 12V unit combined with a solar-plus-battery system is a world-class solution.
