# The True Cost of Home Security: Cloud Subscriptions vs. Self-Hosted PoE (5-Year TCO)

[Affiliate Disclosure: This post contains affiliate links. If you use these links to buy something we may earn a commission. Thanks.]

In the world of home security, there is a "hidden tax" that most consumers ignore: the cloud subscription.

Brands like Ring, Nest, and Arlo have mastered the art of the low-cost entry. You buy a $150 doorbell, stick it on your house, and feel secure. But then you realize that without a $10/month "protection plan," your expensive camera is little more than a paperweight that can't even save a clip of a porch pirate.

In this analysis, we look at the **Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)** over five years. We’ll compare the industry giants against professional-grade, self-hosted systems like **Reolink** and **Ubiquiti**.

---

## The Cloud Model: Low Upfront, High Bleed
The business model of companies like Amazon (Ring) and Google (Nest) isn't selling cameras—it's selling recurring revenue.

### 5-Year Cost Estimate (4-Camera System):
- **Hardware:** $600 (4 cameras + doorbell)
- **Subscription:** $10/month (Basic) to $30/month (Pro/24-7 recording)
- **5-Year Total:** $1,200 to $2,400

**The Trap:** If you stop paying, you lose your history. You are effectively "renting" your security. Furthermore, you are at the mercy of price hikes. In the last three years, almost every major cloud provider has raised their subscription rates by 20-50%.

---

## The Self-Hosted Model: High Upfront, Zero Bleed
Self-hosted systems, often using **Power over Ethernet (PoE)**, require a higher initial investment in hardware and storage.

### 5-Year Cost Estimate (4-Camera System):
- **Hardware:** $800 (4 Pro-grade 4K cameras + NVR)
- **Storage:** $180 (8TB WD Purple Drive)
- **Subscription:** $0
- **5-Year Total:** $980

**The Benefit:** You own the hardware. You own the data. Whether you have 1 camera or 100, your monthly bill is exactly zero.

---

## The "Break-Even" Point
When does a self-hosted system become "cheaper" than a cloud system?

| System Type | Upfront Cost | Monthly Cost | 2-Year Total | 5-Year Total |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cloud (Ring/Nest)** | $600 | $15 | $960 | $1,500 |
| **Self-Hosted (Reolink)** | $800 | $0 | $800 | $800 |
| **Self-Hosted (Ubiquiti)** | $1,200 | $0 | $1,200 | $1,200 |

As the table shows, a mid-range self-hosted system like **Reolink** is actually cheaper than a cloud system **on day one** when you consider the first year of subscriptions. A premium system like **Ubiquiti** breaks even at the 3-year mark.

---

## The Hidden "Privacy Bonus"
Beyond the dollars and cents, there is the matter of **Data Sovereignty**.
With a cloud system, your private footage is stored on a server owned by a mega-corporation. There have been numerous documented cases of employees accessing footage or police being given access without a warrant.

With a [Self-Hosted Security System](/articles/best-self-hosted-security-2026), your footage never leaves your house. If you want to view it remotely, you can use an encrypted tunnel like **Tailscale** to talk directly to your NVR. No middleman, no metadata, no leaks.

---

## Conclusion: Which should you choose?
- **Choose Cloud if:** You are a renter who cannot drill holes for wires and only plans to stay for 1–2 years.
- **Choose Self-Hosted if:** You are a homeowner looking for the best long-term ROI, superior video quality, and total privacy.
