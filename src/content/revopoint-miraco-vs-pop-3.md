## Introduction
Revopoint has rapidly become the dominant name in accessible, high-precision 3D scanning. But with multiple models hitting the market, many engineers and designers are stuck in a **revopoint miraco vs pop 3 comparison** loop. Both devices offer sub-millimeter accuracy and high-speed data capture, yet they target vastly different user workflows.

In this guide, we break down the hardware, software, and real-world performance differences to help you decide which scanner fits your lab or workshop in 2026.

## Hardware Evolution: Standalone vs. Tethered
The most significant difference between these two scanners is how you use them:
- **Revopoint Miraco:** A standalone device. It features an integrated 6-inch screen, a powerful processor, and up to 32GB of RAM. You don't need a laptop or phone to scan—the device does it all.
- **Revopoint POP 3:** A tethered handheld scanner. It must be connected to a PC via USB or a mobile device via Wi-Fi. While it is lighter and more affordable, your mobility is limited by the cable or the battery life of your connected device.

## Precision and Detail
Both scanners boast an accuracy of up to 0.05 mm, but they excel in different areas:
- **The POP 3** is optimized for small-to-medium objects. If you are scanning jewelry, small mechanical parts, or figurines, the POP 3 often provides a smoother, more refined mesh for its size class.
- **The Miraco** is a "Big and Small" beast. It uses a different sensor array that allows it to switch between Near-mode (for small details) and Far-mode (for large objects like body panels or furniture) seamlessly.

## Performance Analysis: Speed and Software
The Miraco's onboard processing allows for real-world scanning speeds that are hard to beat. Because the processing happens locally on optimized hardware, there is less lag between movement and the point cloud appearing on the screen.

However, the **Revopoint POP 3** benefits from the power of your workstation. If you have a high-end PC, the Revo Scan software can often handle complex global optimizations slightly faster during the post-processing phase.

## Pros & Cons

### Revopoint POP 3
**Pros**

**Cons**

- Struggling with very large objects.

## Final Verdict
If your work requires you to move around large objects or scan in environments where a laptop is a burden (like a garage or outdoors), the **Revopoint Miraco** is worth every penny of the premium.

If your scanning is primarily done at a desk and you focus on individual parts or hobbyist projects, the **Revopoint POP 3** offers professional-grade precision at a much lower entry point.
