# Sovereign Science: The $1,000 Home Bio-Lab

The democratization of biotechnology is no longer a futuristic concept. With the expiration of key patents and the rise of the open-source hardware movement, building a functional biology lab in a home office is now feasible for under $1,000.

## 1. The Core Pillar: Thermal Cycling (PCR)
The **Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR)** is the "search and replace" of biology. While professional units cost $5,000+, an **OpenPCR** kit or a used **miniPCR** unit can be acquired for $400-$600. This allows you to amplify specific DNA sequences for analysis.

## 2. Separation Physics: The Centrifuge
A centrifuge is essential for separating components by density. For amateur labs, a used clinical centrifuge or a high-quality 3D-printed unit with a brushless motor can handle the 10,000+ RPM required for DNA extraction.
- **Budget:** $150 (Used/DIY)

## 3. Visualization: Gel Electrophoresis
To see your results, you need gel electrophoresis. This relies on the physics of electrical charge moving through a porous matrix (agarose). A DIY power supply and a transilluminator (blue light) can be built for under $100 using off-the-shelf components.
