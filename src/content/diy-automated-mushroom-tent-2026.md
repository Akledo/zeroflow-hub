# How to Build an Automated Mushroom Grow Tent in 5 Steps (2026)

*Affiliate Disclosure: This post contains affiliate links. If you purchase through these links, we may earn a commission at no additional cost to you.*

Gourmet mushrooms like Lion's Mane, Oyster, and Shiitake are notoriously picky. They require high humidity (80-90%), consistent fresh air exchange (FAE), and stable temperatures. Managing these variables manually is a recipe for burnout and failed harvests.

The solution? An **automated mushroom grow tent**—often called a "Martha Tent." In this guide, we’ll show you how to build a pro-sumer grade setup that handles the heavy lifting for you, ensuring consistent yields of medicinal and gourmet fungi.

## Step 1: The Tent and Shelving
Start with a standard 4-tier mini greenhouse. These are inexpensive and provide the perfect vertical footprint for home cultivation.
*   **Recommendation:** A standard PVC-covered 4-tier greenhouse (approx. 27" x 19" x 63").
*   **Pro Tip:** Line the bottom with a heavy-duty tray or pond liner to catch condensation.

## Step 2: Humidity Automation
Mushrooms need "cool mist," not heat. An ultrasonic humidifier is essential.
*   **The Setup:** Place a large-capacity ultrasonic humidifier outside the tent. Connect it to the tent using a 4-inch flexible dryer duct.
*   **The Controller:** Use an **Inkbird IHC-200 Humidistat**. Place the sensor inside the tent (mid-level). Plug the humidifier into the controller. Set your target to 85% with a 5% differential.

## Step 3: Fresh Air Exchange (FAE) and CO2 Management
Mushrooms breathe oxygen and exhale CO2. If CO2 levels get too high, your mushrooms will grow long, spindly stems and small caps.
*   **The Setup:** Install a 4-inch inline duct fan at the top of the tent to pull air *out*. This creates negative pressure, pulling fresh air in through the gaps in the bottom.
*   **Automation:** Connect the fan to a repeat cycle timer. Set it to run for 2-3 minutes every hour (adjust based on your CO2 monitor readings).

## Step 4: Temperature and Lighting
While most gourmet mushrooms grow well at room temperature (65-75°F), consistent lighting helps guide the direction of growth.
*   **Lighting:** Use a simple LED strip light (6000K daylight spectrum) on a 12/12 timer. Mushrooms don't photosynthesize, but the light triggers "pinning."
*   **Temperature:** If your grow space is cold, use a waterproof seedling heat mat on the bottom shelf, controlled by an Inkbird ITC-308 Temperature Controller.

## Step 5: Sealing and Drainage
High humidity leads to standing water. You must manage this to prevent mold and bacteria.
*   **Sealing:** Use waterproof tape to secure the ducting and seal any large gaps in the PVC cover.
*   **Drainage:** Ensure the shelves are rust-proof (stainless steel or plastic-coated). Wipe down the interior with a 10% bleach solution or 70% ISO between grows.

## Summary Checklist: The Hardware You Need
*   4-Tier Mini Greenhouse
*   Ultrasonic Humidifier
*   **Inkbird IHC-200 Humidity Controller**
*   4-Inch Inline Duct Fan
*   Repeat Cycle Timer
*   6000K LED Strip Lights

## Conclusion: Set it and Forget It
Once you’ve calibrated your **automated mushroom grow tent**, your only job is to rotate your substrate blocks and harvest the results. Automation removes the variance of human error, allowing you to focus on the science of mycology rather than the chore of misting.
