# Don't Kill Your Cells: LFP Winter Survival

Lithium Iron Phosphate (LFP) batteries are incredible, but they have a fatal flaw: **Lithium Plating**. If you charge an LFP cell below 0°C (32°F), lithium ions "plate" the surface of the anode instead of intercalating into it, causing permanent capacity loss and potential fire hazards.

## 1. The Physics of Insulation
Air is a poor conductor of heat (R-value). A simple DIY insulated box using 2-inch polyisocyanurate foam can retain enough self-generated heat (from internal resistance during discharge) to keep the bank above freezing.

## 2. Active Heating Mats
For extreme climates, active 12V heating mats with integrated thermostats are mandatory. These should be placed *between* cells or along the bottom of the enclosure to ensure even heat distribution.

## 3. The Sodium-Ion Alternative
If your environment is consistently sub-zero, consider [Sodium-Ion technology](https://github.com/Akledo/zeroflow-hub/tree/writer/wave3-batteries), which can be charged safely down to -20°C without plating risks.

## 4. BMS Low-Temp Cutoff
Never use a battery in winter that doesn't have a **BMS with low-temp charging protection**. This is your final line of defense.
