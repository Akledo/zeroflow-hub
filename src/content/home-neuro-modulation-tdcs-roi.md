# Home Neuro-Modulation ROI: DIY tDCS for Cognitive Performance

The "Cognitive Performance" market is currently flooded with $1,000+ headsets and $30/month "Brain Optimization" subscriptions. For the ZeroFlow prosumer, these products often hide a simple physical reality behind a glossy UI: **transcranial Direct Current Stimulation (tDCS)**.

tDCS is a well-studied neuro-modulation technique that uses a constant, low intensity current (usually 0.5 to 2.0 mA) delivered via electrodes on the scalp to stimulate specific brain regions.

Is the "Pro" hardware worth the markup, or can a technical user achieve the same results with a precision-engineered DIY rig?

## 1. The Physics: Milliamps and Ohms

The efficacy of tDCS depends on two factors: **Current Precision** and **Montage Accuracy**.

*   **Current Precision:** The brain requires a steady, ripple-free DC current. Cheap, poorly regulated circuits can cause "phosphenes" (visual flashes) or skin irritation.
*   **Montage Accuracy:** This refers to the placement of the anode and cathode. Professional devices use fixed headsets to ensure repeatability, while DIYers use the "10-20 System" (a standardized scalp mapping method).

## 3. Hardware Standards: What to Look For

If you are buying or building, your hardware must meet these prosumer safety standards:
1.  **Current Limiting:** A hard physical limit that prevents the device from ever exceeding 2.5mA, even if a component fails.
2.  **Ramp-up/Ramp-down:** To prevent the "Sting" effect, the current should gradually increase over 30 seconds.
3.  **Impedance Monitoring:** The device should alert you if the sponge electrodes are too dry, as high resistance leads to skin burns.
