# Home Neuro-Modulation: The Physics of tDCS Current and PEMF Field Limitations (2027)

The market for "brain-boosting" headsets is exploding, but the marketing often outpaces the physics. For the technical prosumer, the "Authority Gap" lies in understanding how much energy actually reaches the brain tissue. 

Whether you are using **tDCS** (transcranial Direct Current Stimulation) or **PEMF** (Pulsed Electromagnetic Field) therapy, the effectiveness of the hardware is governed by electromagnetics, not just "vibes."

---

## 1. tDCS: The Challenge of Shunting and Impedance
tDCS hardware (like the **Fisher Wallace** or **Flow Neuroscience** devices) works by passing a low-level constant current (1-2mA) between two electrodes.

*   **The Physics of the Scalp:** The human scalp and skull are surprisingly good insulators. 
*   **The Shunting Effect:** A significant portion of the electrical current (up to 60-80%) never reaches the brain. Instead, it travels along the skin and scalp between the electrodes. This is known as "shunting."
*   **Technical Spec - Skin Impedance:** High-quality tDCS hardware must use saline-soaked sponges or conductive gels to lower skin impedance. If your headset doesn't monitor impedance in real-time, the "dosage" of current reaching your neurons is essentially a guess.
*   **Signal-to-Noise:** To achieve neural "priming," the current must be stable. Low-end DIY units often have "ripple" in their power delivery, which increases the risk of phosphenes (seeing flashes of light) without providing cognitive benefit.

---

## 2. PEMF: The Inverse Square Law
PEMF devices (like the **NeoRhythm** or **Omni PEMF**) use magnetic induction. They don't pass current through the skin; they create a magnetic field that induces a tiny electrical current *inside* the brain tissue.

*   **The Inverse Square Law:** The strength of a magnetic field drops off precipitously with distance. In physics, the intensity is inversely proportional to the square of the distance from the source ($1/d^2$).
*   **The Limitation:** For a PEMF headband, if the coil is even 1cm away from the target brain region (e.g., due to thick hair or poor fit), the field strength is radically reduced.
*   **Entrainment:** PEMF relies on "Frequency Following Response." The hardware must output extremely precise pulse rates (e.g., 40Hz for Gamma focus) to encourage the brain to sync its own electrical activity to that rhythm.

## 4. The "Montage" and Electrical Flow
In tDCS, where you place the electrodes is called a **Montage**. 
*   **Anodal (+) vs. Cathodal (-):** Anodal stimulation generally increases neuronal excitability (focus/learning), while Cathodal stimulation decreases it (calming/sleep).
*   **Hardware Gap:** Generic headsets often have fixed electrode positions. Professional-grade home hardware allows for "Montage customization," enabling the user to target specific regions like the Dorsolateral Prefrontal Cortex (DLPFC).
