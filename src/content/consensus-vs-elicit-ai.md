## Introduction
For academics and researchers in 2026, the volume of scientific literature is overwhelming. Conducting a **systematic review** used to take months, but new AI-powered tools are slashing that time significantly. Two of the most popular platforms in this space are **Consensus** and **Elicit**.

In this comparison, we explore which tool is best for your specific research needs, focusing on accuracy, data extraction, and overall workflow efficiency.

## What is Consensus?
**Consensus** is a search engine that uses artificial intelligence to find answers in scientific research. Instead of giving you a list of links, Consensus reads the papers and provides a synthesized answer based on the peer-reviewed literature.

### Key Features of Consensus
- **Consensus Meter:** Shows the percentage of papers that agree, disagree, or are neutral regarding your search query.
- **Synthesized Answers:** Provides clear, one-sentence summaries of the current scientific consensus.
- **Quality Filters:** Easily filter results by journal quality, citation count, and study type.

## What is Elicit?
**Elicit** is an AI research assistant designed to automate the most tedious parts of a literature review. It goes beyond simple search by helping you extract specific data points from dozens of papers simultaneously.

### Key Features of Elicit
- **Data Extraction Tables:** Automatically extract information such as sample size, methodology, and outcomes.
- **Paper Summarization:** Provides concise summaries of the main findings.
- **Related Paper Discovery:** Helps you find relevant literature you might have missed.

## Performance Analysis: Systematic Reviews

### Search and Synthesis
**Consensus** excels at the "search and synthesis" phase. If you have a specific question, Consensus gives you a bird's-eye view of the entire field almost instantly.

### Data Extraction
**Elicit** is the clear winner for the "data extraction" phase. For a systematic review, you often need to compare the results of 20+ studies. Elicit's ability to pull that data into a single table saves hours of manual work.

## Final Verdict

- **Choose Consensus** if you need a fast way to find the scientific consensus on a topic or are in the early stages of a project.
- **Choose Elicit** if you are conducting a rigorous systematic review and need to extract and compare data from a large number of studies.
